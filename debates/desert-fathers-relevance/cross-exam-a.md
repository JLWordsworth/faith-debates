# Cross-Examination Questions for Opponent

**Debater A (Pro-Relevance) to Debater B (Anti-Relevance)**

## Question 1

You argue that the desert fathers retreated from the world in a dangerous withdrawal, but how do you reconcile this critique with Jesus' own pattern of withdrawing to wilderness places for prayer and preparation (Mark 1:35, Luke 5:16)? If our Lord Himself prioritized solitude with the Father, on what basis do you label this same practice as inherently dangerous rather than as a necessary rhythm of spiritual formation?

## Question 2

You express concern about desert asceticism being potentially unbiblical, yet Paul explicitly references athletes exercising self-control and disciplining their bodies (1 Corinthians 9:24-27), and the author of Hebrews calls us to lay aside every weight and sin (Hebrews 12:1). How do you distinguish between "dangerous asceticism" and the biblical call to self-denial without rendering the latter meaningless?
